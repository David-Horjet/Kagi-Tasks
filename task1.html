Classic Beef Stew Recipe

Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Season beef with salt and pepper, then brown on all sides.

Add onions and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in garlic and cook for another minute.

Sprinkle flour over the meat and vegetables, stirring to coat everything evenly.

Gradually add beef broth while stirring to prevent lumps from forming.

Add tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil.

Add potatoes, carrots, and celery to the pot. Season with thyme and additional salt and pepper.

Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours until meat is tender.

In the last 30 minutes, add frozen peas and pearl onions if using.

Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Remove bay leaves before serving.

For a thicker stew, mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with cold water to make a slurry.

Stir the cornstarch slurry into the stew and simmer for 5-10 minutes until thickened.

Garnish with fresh parsley before serving. This stew pairs perfectly with crusty bread.

Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for up to 3 months.

When reheating, add a little broth or water if the stew has thickened too much.

For extra flavor, consider adding a splash of red wine when browning the meat.

This recipe serves 6-8 people and is perfect for cold winter evenings.
